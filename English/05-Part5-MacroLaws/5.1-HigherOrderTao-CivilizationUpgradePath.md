# Chapter 12 Higher Order Tao: Path of Civilization Upgrade

## 12.1 Higher Order Tao Theorems: Possibility of Active Construction

### Core Proposition

**Higher Order Tao is an active construction aimed at long-term group survival after recognizing the laws, without violating any underlying axioms.**

### Higher Order Tao Theorem System

#### 1. Compliance Theorem

**Higher Order Tao does not deny underlying laws such as survival needs, limited resources, and group evolution, but reconstructs resource allocation and power rules.**

- **Theoretical Basis**: Meta-Axiom System
- **Core Logic**: Achieving better resource allocation through institutional design while respecting objective laws
- **Practical Significance**: Avoiding utopian fantasies, ensuring feasibility of theory

#### 2. Anti-Predation Theorem

**Abolishing "gratuitous possession by monopolizing means of production/power" through institutions, eliminating institutionalized predation within humans, retaining only equal exchange and reasonable division of labor benefits.**

- **Theoretical Basis**: Predation for Profit Axiom
- **Core Logic**: Curbing predatory behavior through institutional design, ensuring fair distribution of resources
- **Practical Significance**: Eliminating the endogenous root cause of the system, achieving sustainable development

#### 3. Group Advantage Theorem

**Investing incremental resources in universal education, medical care, innovation, infrastructure, and security, resulting in overall vitality, risk resistance, and long-term survival quality far higher than lower-order order.**

- **Theoretical Basis**: Collaborative Value-Add Axiom
- **Core Logic**: Improving overall quality and ability of the group through public investment
- **Practical Significance**: Releasing the innovation potential of the group, enhancing the overall competitiveness of the system

#### 4. Anti-Solidification Theorem

**Class mobility is based solely on individual ability, labor, and contribution, blocking intergenerational monopoly advantages and maintaining stable mobility.**

- **Theoretical Basis**: Reverse Design of Class Solidification Theorem
- **Core Logic**: Establishing fair competition mechanisms, ensuring equal opportunities
- **Practical Significance**: Maintaining social vitality, avoiding system rigidity caused by class solidification

#### 5. Cycle Escape Theorem

**Eliminating the endogenous collapse motivation of "infinite upgrade of predation", resource allocation becomes sustainable, no longer falling into the cycle of Lower Order Tao.**

- **Theoretical Basis**: Reverse Design of Endogenous Cycle Theorem
- **Core Logic**: Achieving balanced energy flow through institutional design
- **Practical Significance**: Breaking through historical cycle laws, achieving continuous development of civilization

## 12.3 Practical Exploration of Civilization Upgrade

### Historical Practice

#### Primitive Commune
- **Characteristics**: Primitive communism, resource sharing
- **Significance**: Early human symbiotic practice
- **Limitation**: Low productivity level, unable to achieve large-scale collaboration

#### Socialist Exploration
- **Characteristics**: Public ownership of means of production, planned economy
- **Significance**: First large-scale attempt by humans to eliminate institutionalized predation
- **Challenge**: Various problems and difficulties encountered during exploration

#### Cooperative Economy
- **Characteristics**: Workers jointly own and manage means of production
- **Significance**: Symbiotic practice of grassroots economic organizations
- **Advantage**: Strong flexibility, adapting to different social environments

### Contemporary Practice

#### Socialism with Chinese Characteristics
- **Characteristics**: Public ownership as main body, multiple forms of ownership developing together
- **Achievement**: Rapid economic development, historic achievements in poverty alleviation
- **Exploration**: Innovative practice in constructing symbiotic order

#### Nordic Welfare State
- **Characteristics**: High welfare, high tax, social fairness
- **Achievement**: Social stability, high living standard
- **Limitation**: Facing challenges such as slowing economic growth, welfare sustainability

#### Cooperative Movement
- **Characteristics**: Workers voluntarily unite, democratic management
- **Achievement**: Successful in agriculture, handicraft and other fields
- **Potential**: Can serve as important component of symbiotic economy
