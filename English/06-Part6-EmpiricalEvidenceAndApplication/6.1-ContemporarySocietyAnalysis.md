# Chapter 14 Contemporary Society Analysis

## 14.1 Analysis of Contemporary Capitalism

### Case Background

Contemporary capitalism is the modern form of Lower Order Tao. Starting from the Industrial Revolution, it has gone through stages such as free capitalism, monopoly capitalism, and state monopoly capitalism, and is currently in the stage of financial capitalism.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Social Trophic Level Structure

1. **First Trophic Level · Production Layer**:
   - Industrial workers, service industry practitioners, farmers.
   - Bottom white-collar workers, temporary workers, gig workers.

2. **Second Trophic Level · Transmission Layer**:
   - Middle managers, technical personnel, professionals.
   - Small and medium business owners, freelancers.

3. **Third Trophic Level · Accumulation Layer**:
   - Large business owners, financial oligarchs, top capitalists.
   - Political elites, media tycoons.

#### Operating Mechanism

1. **Resource Flow**:
   - The production layer creates surplus value.
   - Surplus value flows upward through wages, profits, interest, etc.
   - Eventually enriched in the hands of financial capital and large enterprises.

2. **Meme Domestication**:
   - Neoliberal ideology: Emphasizing market freedom, individualism.
   - Consumerism: Achieving self-worth through consumption.
   - Successology: Emphasizing personal struggle, ignoring structural factors.

3. **Placebo Channels**:
   - Education system: Providing opportunities for upward mobility.
   - Entrepreneurship myth: Promoting successful cases of starting from scratch.
   - Social welfare: Providing basic living security, alleviating social contradictions.

### Contemporary Characteristics

1. **Financialization**:
   - Financial capital dominates the economy, real economy relatively declines.
   - Financial speculation becomes an important source of profit.
   - Debt economy model, exacerbating economic instability.

2. **Globalization**:
   - Global flow of capital, technology, and labor.
   - Global expansion of multinational corporations.
   - Formation of global value chains.

3. **Technological Revolution**:
   - Development of information technology and artificial intelligence.
   - Impact of automation on employment.
   - Widening of digital divide.

4. **Social Polarization**:
   - Wealth gap continues to widen.
   - Middle class shrinks.
   - Social mobility declines.

### Theoretical Verification

- **Unidirectional Resource Flow Theorem**: Surplus value flows from the production layer to the accumulation layer, verifying the law of unidirectional resource flow.
- **Technological Efficiency Theorem**: Increments created by technological progress are prioritized and monopolized by the top layer, exacerbating wealth gap.
- **Endogenous Cycle Theorem**: Cyclical outbreaks of capitalist economic crises verify the cycle law of Lower Order Tao.

## 14.2 Exploration of Socialism with Chinese Characteristics

### Case Background

Socialism with Chinese characteristics is an exploration of Higher Order Tao. Starting from the reform and opening up, it has experienced the transformation from a planned economy to a market economy, and is currently in the new era of socialism with Chinese characteristics.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Social Trophic Level Structure

1. **First Trophic Level · Production Layer**:
   - Industrial workers, farmers, service industry practitioners.
   - Bottom laborers.

2. **Second Trophic Level · Transmission Layer**:
   - Middle managers, technical personnel, professionals.
   - Small and medium business owners, individual industrial and commercial households.

3. **Third Trophic Level · Accumulation Layer**:
   - Large business owners, entrepreneurs.
   - Political elites, technological elites.

#### Operating Mechanism

1. **Resource Flow**:
   - The production layer creates surplus value.
   - Surplus value flows through wages, profits, taxes, etc.
   - Part of surplus value is used for public undertakings such as education, medical care, and infrastructure through public finance.

2. **Meme Domestication**:
   - Core socialist values: Emphasizing collectivism, fairness and justice.
   - Chinese Dream: Achieving the great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation.
   - Common Prosperity: Eliminating poverty, achieving common prosperity for all people.

3. **Positive Cycle**:
   - Public Investment: Investing in education, medical care, infrastructure, etc.
   - Social Security: Establishing a social security system covering all people.
   - Poverty Alleviation: Eliminating absolute poverty.

### Exploration Achievements

1. **Economic Development**:
   - Economy continues to grow rapidly, becoming the world's second largest economy.
   - Scientific and technological innovation capabilities continue to improve.
   - Infrastructure construction has made huge achievements.

2. **Social Progress**:
   - Historic achievements in poverty alleviation, eliminating absolute poverty.
   - Social security system constantly improved.
   - Education and medical levels significantly improved.

3. **Institutional Innovation**:
   - Public ownership as the main body, multiple forms of ownership developing together.
   - Distribution according to work as the main body, multiple distribution methods coexisting.
   - Socialist market economy system.

### Theoretical Verification

- **Anti-Predation Theorem**: Through institutional design, curbing institutionalized predation, achieving common prosperity.
- **Group Advantage Theorem**: Through public investment, improving the overall quality and ability of the group.
- **Anti-Solidification Theorem**: Through education fairness, social security and other measures, promoting social mobility.

## 14.3 Evolution of Social Trophic Levels in the Technological Era

### Case Background

The technological era, represented by information technology, artificial intelligence, biotechnology, etc., is profoundly changing social structure and resource allocation methods.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Changes in Social Trophic Level Structure

1. **First Trophic Level · Production Layer**:
   - Traditional industrial workers decrease.
   - Digital laborers, platform workers increase.
   - Gig economy practitioners increase.

2. **Second Trophic Level · Transmission Layer**:
   - Technical white-collar workers, data analysts increase.
   - Traditional middle managers decrease.
   - Freelancers, creators increase.

3. **Third Trophic Level · Accumulation Layer**:
   - Tech giants, platform owners.
   - Data capitalists, algorithm designers.
   - Top technical experts.

#### Changes in Operating Mechanism

1. **Resource Flow**:
   - Data becomes an important factor of production.
   - Commission model of platform economy.
   - Impact of algorithms on resource allocation.

2. **Meme Domestication**:
   - Algorithmic Recommendation: Shaping user cognition and behavior.
   - Platform Rules: Controlling user behavior boundaries.
   - Digital Identity: Constructing user's digital image.

3. **Technology Empowerment and Digital Divide**:
   - Technology Empowerment: Improving production efficiency, creating new employment opportunities.
   - Digital Divide: Differences in technology usage capabilities lead to new social differentiation.

### Challenges and Opportunities

1. **Challenges**:
   - Changes in employment structure, reduction of traditional jobs.
   - Data monopoly, platform power too large.
   - Privacy protection, personal data over-collected.
   - Algorithmic bias, exacerbating social inequality.

2. **Opportunities**:
   - Technological innovation, improving production efficiency.
   - Remote work, increasing work flexibility.
   - Digital economy, creating new economic growth points.
   - Education innovation, improving human capital.

### Theoretical Verification

- **Technological Efficiency Theorem**: Increments created by technological progress are prioritized and monopolized by tech giants.
- **Unidirectional Resource Flow Theorem**: Data and wealth concentrate towards platform owners.
- **Symbiotic Possibility**: Technological progress provides new possibilities for realizing Higher Order Tao.

## 14.4 Common Challenges of Contemporary Society

### 1. Environmental Crisis

- Climate Change: Global temperature rise, increase in extreme weather events.
- Resource Depletion: Shortage of fossil energy, water resources, etc.
- Ecological Destruction: Decrease in biodiversity, degradation of ecosystems.

### 2. Social Inequality

- Wealth Gap: Wealth gap continues to widen globally.
- Opportunity Inequality: Unequal distribution of opportunities such as education and employment.
- Intergenerational Inequality: Resource allocation gap between generations.

### 3. Technological Risks

- Artificial Intelligence Ethics: Fairness and transparency of AI decision-making.
- Network Security: Data leakage, cyber attacks.
- Technological Unemployment: Impact of automation on employment.

### 4. Global Governance Dilemma

- International Cooperation: Global issues require international cooperation.
- Hegemonism: Unilateral actions of individual countries.
- Global Justice: Fairness of global resource allocation.

## 14.5 Theoretical Significance

### Diagnosis of Contemporary Society

- **System Diagnosis**: Diagnosing the health of contemporary society through social trophic level analysis.
- **Risk Warning**: Identifying risk factors for system collapse.
- **Development Direction**: Pointing out the direction of social development.

### Guidance for Policy Making

- **Institutional Design**: Designing institutions conducive to symbiotic development.
- **Policy Focus**: Focusing on education fairness, social security, technological innovation.
- **Global Governance**: Promoting the construction of a community with a shared future for mankind.

### Future Inspirations

- **Technology and Institution**: Technological progress requires the cooperation of institutional innovation.
- **Fairness and Efficiency**: Achieving a dynamic balance between fairness and efficiency.
- **Global Cooperation**: Addressing global challenges requires global cooperation.

## 14.6 Conclusion

Contemporary society is in a critical period of evolution from Lower Order Tao to Higher Order Tao. Through theoretical analysis, we can better understand the operating mechanism of contemporary society, identify challenges and opportunities, and provide references for building a more fair, efficient, and sustainable social structure.

Technological progress provides new possibilities for the upgrade of human civilization, but also brings new challenges. Only through institutional innovation, cultural transformation, and global cooperation can humans fully utilize the dividends of technological progress and achieve the evolutionary leap from Lower Order Tao to Higher Order Tao.

The development of contemporary society proves that Higher Order Tao is not an unattainable ideal, but a realistic choice based on objective laws. By recognizing and transcending the limitations of Lower Order Tao, humans can build a more harmonious, fair, and sustainable social form and achieve the continuous development of civilization.
