# Chapter 7 Practical Path: Void Boat Thinking and Self-Transcendence

## 7.1 Void Boat Thinking: A Method to Transcend Obsession

### Core Proposition

**Void Boat Thinking is a way of thinking that transcends individual obsession and achieves cognitive freedom.**

Void Boat Thinking is not passive avoidance of the world, but a positive cognitive attitude and behavioral strategy:

### Metaphor of the Void Boat

- **Source**: The concept of "Void Boat" (Xu Zhou) in Zhuangzi's "Xiaoyaoyou".
- **Meaning**: Like an empty boat, drifting with the flow without a fixed direction or goal, yet maintaining inner peace.
- **Essence**: Transcending individual obsession, conforming to natural laws, while maintaining inner autonomy.

### Core Principles of Void Boat Thinking

1. **Non-Attachment**: Not attached to specific goals, identities, or concepts.
2. **Conformity**: Conforming to natural and social laws, rather than forcibly opposing them.
3. **Inner Stability**: Maintaining inner peace and autonomy, undisturbed by the external environment.
4. **Flexibility**: Adjusting flexibly according to environmental changes, rather than being rigid and stubborn.

### Practical Methods of Void Boat Thinking

#### 1. Obsession Identification

- **Self-Observation**: Observing one's own thinking patterns and behavioral motivations.
- **Obsession Analysis**: Identifying which concepts and goals are truly important and which are instilled by society.
- **Value Reconstruction**: Reconstructing personal values based on real needs.

#### 2. Cognitive Dissociation

- **Sense of Distance**: Maintaining distance from one's own thoughts and emotions.
- **Observer Perspective**: Examining one's own thinking as an observer.
- **Non-Judgment**: Not judging one's own thoughts and emotions.
- **Acceptance**: Accepting one's own thoughts and emotions, not fighting against them.

#### 3. Conforming to Nature

- **Law Cognition**: Understanding the laws of nature and society.
- **Going with the Flow**: Acting within the scope allowed by laws.
- **Resource Utilization**: Making full use of existing resources, rather than forcibly creating.
- **Rhythm Control**: Grasping the timing and rhythm of action.

#### 4. Inner Autonomy

- **Core Values**: Clarifying one's core values and goals.
- **Boundary Setting**: Setting personal boundaries and bottom lines.
- **Energy Management**: Rationally allocating one's time and energy.
- **Self-Care**: Taking care of one's physical and mental health.

## 7.2 Personal Practice Framework: From Cognitive Awakening to Behavioral Change

### Core Proposition

**The Personal Practice Framework is a complete path from cognitive awakening to behavioral change.**

### Four Stages of the Practice Framework

#### 1. Cognitive Awakening

**Goal**: Realize the existence of meme domestication and start questioning concepts instilled by society.

**Specific Actions**:
- **Information Input**: Read relevant theories and understand the mechanism of meme domestication.
- **Self-Reflection**: Reflect on one's own values and behavioral patterns.
- **Reality Observation**: Observe social phenomena and identify manifestations of meme domestication.
- **Cognitive Shock**: Experience cognitive shock and start questioning original concepts.

#### 2. Method Mastery

**Goal**: Learn and master cognitive decoding tools and methods.

**Specific Actions**:
- **Lingshi Framework**: Learn and apply the four steps of the Lingshi Framework.
- **Void Boat Thinking**: Cultivate the core principles of Void Boat Thinking.
- **Energy Management**: Learn methods of energy tracking and management.
- **Cognitive Tools**: Master cognitive tools such as critical thinking and systems thinking.

#### 3. Practical Application

**Goal**: Apply theoretical knowledge to daily life and start changing behavioral patterns.

**Specific Actions**:
- **Small-Scale Attempts**: Start trying new behavioral patterns from small things in daily life.
- **Feedback Adjustment**: Adjust methods based on practice results.
- **Habit Formation**: Cultivate new behavioral patterns into habits.
- **Effect Assessment**: Assess practice effects and summarize experiences and lessons.

#### 4. Self-Transcendence

**Goal**: Achieve cognitive sovereignty and reach the realm of self-transcendence.

**Specific Actions**:
- **Cognitive Integration**: Integrate learned theories and methods into one's own cognitive system.
- **Behavioral Autonomy**: Decide behavior autonomously based on cognitive sovereignty.
- **Meaning Construction**: Construct a personal meaning system.
- **Continuous Evolution**: Constantly improve cognitive ability and realm.

## 7.3 Cognitive Sovereignty: The Ultimate Goal of Anti-Domestication

### Core Proposition

**Cognitive sovereignty is the ultimate goal of the individual and the foundation for achieving true freedom.**

### Manifestations of Cognitive Sovereignty

1. **Thinking Autonomy**: Able to think independently, unrestricted by concepts instilled by society.
2. **Behavioral Autonomy**: Able to decide behavior autonomously, swaying by external pressure.
3. **Emotional Autonomy**: Able to manage one's own emotions, not controlled by emotions.
4. **Meaning Autonomy**: Able to construct one's own meaning system, not relying on external values.

### Signs of Realizing Cognitive Sovereignty

- **Clear Self-Cognition**: Understanding one's own needs, values, and goals.
- **Independent Judgment Ability**: Able to independently analyze and judge information.
- **Flexible Adaptability**: Able to adapt to environmental changes and maintain inner stability.
- **Creative Thinking Ability**: Able to create new cognitive and behavioral patterns.

### Social Value of Cognitive Sovereignty

- **Personal Happiness**: Holders of cognitive sovereignty can obtain higher happiness and satisfaction.
- **Social Innovation**: Cognitive sovereignty is the source of social innovation.
- **Civilization Progress**: The popularization of cognitive sovereignty promotes the progress of civilization.
- **Group Survival**: Cognitive sovereignty contributes to the long-term survival of the group.
