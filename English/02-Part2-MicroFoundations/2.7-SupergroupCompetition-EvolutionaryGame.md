## 5.7 Supergroup Competition: Evolutionary Game from Zero-Sum to Non-Zero-Sum

### Core Proposition

**The competition between supergroups has gradually evolved from a primitive zero-sum game to a non-zero-sum game, with win-win cooperation becoming a higher-dimensional survival strategy.**

In the framework of Gene Group Evolution Theory, the competition between different civilizations and nations is essentially a survival game of Gene-Culture Complexes, and its form has undergone a profound evolution from violent conquest to institutional competition, and from resource contention to rule-making.

---

### I. Evolutionary Levels of Supergroups

#### Level 1: Biological Gene Competition

- **Characteristics**: Violent expansion aimed at survival space and resource acquisition.
- **Typical Cases**: Invasion of agrarian civilizations by nomadic peoples, territorial disputes in the colonial era.
- **Logic**: Law of the jungle, winner takes all.

#### Level 2: Cultural Meme Competition

- **Characteristics**: Ideological competition centered on values and institutional export.
- **Typical Cases**: US-Soviet confrontation during the Cold War, religious spread and Crusades.
- **Logic**: Survival of the fittest, civilization assimilation.

#### Level 3: Rule System Competition

- **Characteristics**: Institutional competition dominated by setting international rules and standards.
- **Typical Cases**: WTO rules, financial system standards, technology ethics norms.
- **Logic**: Leaders set rules, sharers gain dividends.

---

### II. Evolutionary Logic of Zero-Sum Game

#### Zero-Sum Competition Driven by Resource Scarcity

Under conditions of limited resources, the survival of supergroups is essentially a zero-sum game:

- **Land Resources**: Agrarian civilizations' struggle for arable land led to wars and conquests.
- **Energy Resources**: Industrial civilizations' control over fossil fuels triggered geopolitical conflicts.
- **Human Resources**: Cultural conflicts and social problems caused by labor migration.

#### Essence of Civilization Conflict

Huntington pointed out that the "Clash of Civilizations" is essentially the survival competition of different Gene-Culture Complexes:

- The "All-under-Heaven View" of Chinese civilization achieved continuity across millennia through inclusive identity.
- The Roman Empire collapsed due to the failure of the "Citizenship Meme".
- Islamic civilization maintained cross-regional cohesion through religious identity.

---

### III. Evolutionary Path of Non-Zero-Sum Game

#### Structure from Confrontation to Cooperation

1. **Interdependence**: Industrial chain interdependence formed by economic globalization.
2. **Common Threats**: Global issues such as climate warming, pandemics, and AI risks.
3. **Institutional Innovation**: International organizations provide cooperation platforms and rule frameworks.

#### Typical Patterns of Non-Zero-Sum Game

**Win-Win Cooperation Type**:

- EU Integration: Member states achieve common development through institutional cession.
- Belt and Road Initiative: Infrastructure connectivity creates incremental wealth.
- Carbon Trading System: Internalizing environmental costs as economic incentives.

**Competition-Cooperation Coexistence Type**:

- China-US Economic and Trade Relations: Bilateral relations where competition and cooperation coexist.
- Technology Competition: Strategic competition in 5G, AI, etc., accompanied by cooperation.
- Talent Flow: International circulation of intellectual resources.

---

### IV. Strategic Analysis of Evolutionary Game

#### Game Strategy Matrix of Supergroups

| | Cooperation | Confrontation |
|---|---|---|
| **Cooperation** | Win-Win (Building a Community with a Shared Future for Mankind) | I Win You Lose (Unilateralism) |
| **Confrontation** | You Win I Lose (Compromise for Peace) | Lose-Lose (Thucydides Trap) |

#### Optimal Strategy: Differentiated Coexistence

- **Avoid Homogenized Competition**: Different civilizations leverage comparative advantages.
- **Establish Co-opetition Boundaries**: Compete on core interests, cooperate on public goods.
- **Create Incremental Space**: Expand common interests through technological and institutional innovation.

---

### V. Evolutionary Trends of Supergroups

#### 1. Transformation from Violence to Institutions

- The role of military power shifts from conquest to maintaining order.
- Economic power exerts influence through rule-making.
- Soft power becomes the core dimension of competition.

#### 2. Evolution from Closed to Open

- Supergroup boundaries gradually blur.
- Immigration and cultural exchange promote gene-culture fusion.
- Transnational issues require multilateral cooperation to solve.

#### 3. Upgrade from Survival to Development

- After basic survival needs are met, the right to development becomes the main appeal.
- The dimension of civilization competition shifts from material to cultural and institutional.
- Issues of common human destiny become prominent.

---

### VI. Typical Cases of Contemporary Supergroup Competition

#### USA: Hegemony Maintenance and Rule Leadership

- Building a security network through military alliances.
- Setting international economic rules through institutions like IMF and WTO.
- Exporting values through cultural products (Hollywood, Silicon Valley).

#### China: Peaceful Development and Common Prosperity

- Proposing the concept of "Community with a Shared Future for Mankind".
- Building infrastructure networks through the Belt and Road Initiative.
- Emphasizing civilization diversity and opposing civilization superiority.

#### EU: Experiment of Supranational Governance

- Sovereignty cession to achieve integration depth.
- Common market creates economic efficiency.
- Welfare state provides a social security template.

---

### Conclusion: Ultimate Direction of Evolutionary Game

The evolution of supergroup competition from zero-sum to non-zero-sum is an inevitable choice for Gene-Culture Complexes to maximize fitness in higher dimensions:

- **Zero-Sum Game** is a low-dimensional survival strategy, suitable for the era of resource scarcity.
- **Non-Zero-Sum Game** is a high-dimensional evolutionary strategy, suitable for the mature stage of civilization.
- **Ultimate Direction** is to build a Community with a Shared Future for Mankind, achieving common development while maintaining civilization diversity.

From the perspective of Gene Group Evolution Theory, the highest interest of a supergroup is not to eliminate opponents, but to promote the overall upgrade of human civilization through competition and cooperation.
