# Chapter 4 Behavioral Drivers: Five Instincts and Desire Mechanisms

## 4.1 Five Instinctual Desire Programming

### Core Proposition

**The underlying drive of human behavior is the five instinctual desires, which are the core survival algorithms of genetic programming.**

These instinctual desires are not simple physiological needs, but complex behavioral programs designed by gene groups for their own survival:

### Five Instinctual Desires

#### 1. Survival Desire

- **Core Function**: Maintain carrier endurance
- **Manifestation**: Hunger, thirst, fear of danger, self-protection
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Ensure individual survival, provide carrier for gene transmission
- **Social Impact**: Drive individuals to obtain basic survival resources, foundation of economic activities

#### 2. Reproduction Desire

- **Core Function**: Gene replication core program
- **Manifestation**: Sexual desire, courtship behavior, parenting instinct, parent-child attachment
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Achieve direct transmission of genes, key to population continuation
- **Social Impact**: Shape family structure, gender relations, marriage system

#### 3. Social Desire

- **Core Function**: Construct "Group Safety Net"
- **Manifestation**: Sense of belonging, sense of identity, social needs, group identity
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Improve survival probability through group collaboration, foundation of social formation
- **Social Impact**: Drive individuals to integrate into groups, form social organizations

#### 4. Development Desire

- **Core Function**: Environment pre-adaptation mechanism
- **Manifestation**: Learning skills, resource monopoly, power struggle, status pursuit
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Improve adaptability of individuals and groups, cope with environmental changes
- **Social Impact**: Promote technological progress, social stratification, civilization development

#### 5. Pleasure Desire

- **Core Function**: Behavior reward system
- **Manifestation**: Chemical signals such as dopamine and endorphins, pursuit of pleasurable experiences
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Reinforce behaviors beneficial to survival and reproduction
- **Social Impact**: Drive consumer behavior, entertainment industry, cultural creation

### Hierarchy of Desires

The five desires do not exist in isolation, but form a hierarchical structure:

1. **Foundation Layer**: Survival Desire (Most basic, foundation of other desires)
2. **Core Layer**: Reproduction Desire (Direct drive for gene transmission)
3. **Extension Layer**: Social Desire (Need for group collaboration)
4. **Enhancement Layer**: Development Desire (Improvement of adaptability)
5. **Feedback Layer**: Pleasure Desire (Reward mechanism for behavior)

### Dynamic Balance of Desires

- **Mutual Restraint**: Satisfaction of one desire affects intensity of others
- **Dynamic Adjustment**: According to environmental changes, priority of desires adjusts automatically
- **Individual Differences**: Differences in desire intensity and priority among different individuals

## 4.2 Emotional Feedback Loop

### Core Proposition

**Emotion is a feedback mechanism designed by genes to regulate individual behavior and ensure group interests.**

Emotion is not a random subjective feeling, but a behavioral regulation system programmed by genes:

### Positive Emotion: Reward Mechanism

- **Love and Belonging**: Neural reward for behaviors beneficial to group collaboration
- **Sense of Achievement**: Positive feedback for individual contribution
- **Pleasure**: Direct reward for behaviors beneficial to survival and reproduction
- **Trust**: Emotional mechanism promoting intra-group cooperation

### Negative Emotion: Warning Mechanism

- **Fear**: Warning of dangers threatening survival
- **Shame**: Punishment for behaviors damaging group interests
- **Anxiety**: Warning of potential risks, driving individuals to take action
- **Jealousy**: Perception of unfair resource allocation, maintaining individual rights

### Social Function of Emotion

1. **Behavior Regulation**: Guide individual behavior through emotional experience
2. **Group Coordination**: Emotional resonance promotes behavioral synchronization of group members
3. **Information Transmission**: Emotional expression conveys individual status and need information
4. **Social Control**: Maintain social norms through emotional pressure

## 4.3 Behavioral Evolutionary Logic

### Core Proposition

**The ultimate drive of human behavior is the survival and reproduction of the gene group, and individual behavior is a tool for the gene group to achieve this goal.**

### Behavioral Evolutionary Mechanism

1. **Variation**: Diversity of individual behaviors provides material for selection
2. **Selection**: Behaviors beneficial to gene group survival are retained
3. **Heredity**: Beneficial behaviors are passed to offspring through genes and memes

### Evolutionary Analysis of Behavior Types

#### Altruistic Behavior

- **Kin Altruism**: Altruistic behavior based on genetic similarity
- **Reciprocal Altruism**: Altruistic behavior based on long-term benefit exchange
- **Group Altruism**: Altruistic behavior beneficial to the whole group

#### Selfish Behavior

- **Reasonable Self-Interest**: Behaviors beneficial to individual survival and reproduction
- **Extreme Selfishness**: Selfish behaviors damaging group interests
- **Evolutionary Balance**: Dynamic balance between selfish and altruistic behaviors

### Cultural Shaping of Behavior

- **Meme Filtering**: Cultural rules screen and shape individual behaviors
- **Social Norms**: Guide behavior through rewards and punishments
- **Values**: Shape goals and meaning of behavior

## 4.5 Theoretical Significance

### Implications for Behavioral Science

- **Unified Explanatory Framework**: Integrating behavioral theories of biology, psychology, and sociology
- **Predictive Ability**: Predicting individual and group behaviors based on five desires and emotional mechanisms
- **Intervention Strategy**: Designing more effective social interventions by understanding underlying drivers of behavior

### Significance for Personal Cognition

- **Self-Understanding**: Recognizing underlying drivers of one's own behavior
- **Behavior Management**: Managing own behavior more effectively based on understanding of desire mechanisms
- **Emotional Regulation**: Understanding function of emotions to better regulate emotions

### Implications for Social Policy

- **Incentive Design**: Designing more effective incentive mechanisms based on five desires
- **Social Governance**: Understanding evolutionary logic of behavior to formulate social policies more consistent with human nature
- **Cultural Construction**: Guiding group behavior towards directions beneficial to group survival through meme design
