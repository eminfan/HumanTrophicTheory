# Chapter 3 Human Nature: Dual Coding of Genes and Memes

## 3.1 The Essence of Dual Coding: Gene-Culture Complex

### Core Proposition

**Humans are a life continuation system jointly constituted and co-evolved by genes and cultural memes.**

This proposition overturns the traditional view of human nature, viewing humans as a complex system of dual coding:

### Dual Coding Structure

#### Gene Layer: Underlying Biological Instincts

- **Function**: Setting underlying biological instincts (five major desires), providing the system's energy supply and collaboration prototype
- **Evolution Speed**: Extremely slow, measured in tens of thousands of years
- **Core Mechanism**: Optimizing gene frequency through natural selection
- **Manifestation**: Physiological instincts, emotional reactions, basic behavioral patterns

#### Meme Layer: Upper Social Rules

- **Function**: Constructing a super-biological adaptation system (rules, institutions, culture), expanding survival strategy space
- **Evolution Speed**: Extremely fast, measured in centuries, decades, or even years
- **Core Mechanism**: Optimizing meme transmission through cultural selection
- **Manifestation**: Language, morality, law, institutions, technology

### Synergy and Conflict: Battlefield Model

The relationship between genes and memes is not only "synergy" but also "game". The human body is the **host and battlefield** of these two replicators:

1.  **Synergy (Symbiosis)**: Memes help genes survive better (e.g., agricultural technology memes cause human population explosion).
2.  **Conflict (Parasitism)**: For their own spread, memes may sacrifice gene interests (e.g., celibacy, martyrdom, excessive overtime).
    *   *Note: When a meme can make a person sacrifice reproductive opportunities to spread it, this meme has defeated the gene.*

## 3.2 Extended Phenotype Principle: Biological Basis of Social Systems

### Core Proposition

**Social systems are the "extended body" constructed by gene groups in the cultural layer to maximize their own fitness.**

This principle reveals the essence of social systems:

### Concept of Extended Phenotype

- **Biological Origin**: Proposed by Dawkins in "The Selfish Gene", referring to phenotypic expression of genes beyond individual body boundaries
- **Classic Examples**: Spider webs, beaver dams, bird nests
- **Human Extension**: Human social systems are the extended phenotype of genes, the "collective body" of gene groups

### Extended Phenotype Characteristics of Social Systems

1. **Functional Service**: Social systems serve the survival and reproduction of gene groups
2. **Hereditary Transmission**: Social systems are transmitted through memes, achieving continuous existence of gene groups
3. **Adaptive Evolution**: Social systems evolve with environmental changes, improving gene group fitness

### Theoretical Significance

- **Breaking Binary Opposition**: Eliminating the "biology-culture" binary opposition, viewing social systems as natural extensions of biological evolution
- **Unified Explanatory Framework**: Explaining the origin and function of social phenomena from the gene level
- **Predictive Ability**: Predicting the evolutionary direction of social systems based on gene group survival needs

## 3.3 Negative Entropy Drive Logic: System Energy Demand

### Core Proposition

**The more orderly the system, the greater the negative entropy flow required. The edifice of human civilization is built on the continuous extraction of individual biological energy.**

### Thermodynamic Explanation: The Price of Order

According to dissipative structure theory:
$$ \Delta S_{total} = \Delta S_{system} + \Delta S_{environment} > 0 $$

- In order for the **Social System (System)** to maintain low entropy (order, stability).
- It must discharge more entropy to the **Environment**.
- In sociology, **individuals often become the "Environment" for discharging entropy**.

**Manifestation**:
- **996 Working Hour System**: The system gains orderly profits (low entropy), individuals gain chaotic bodies and minds (high entropy/overwork).
- **War**: The state gains security boundaries (order), soldiers gain death and disability (maximum entropy).

## 3.4 Three Derivative Mechanisms: Dynamic Game of Dual Coding

The interaction between genes and memes in the human body derives three basic social behavior patterns:

### Mechanism 1: Meme Suppression (Superego vs Id)

- **Definition**: Memes forcibly suppress gene instincts to maintain group order.
- **Cases**:
    - **Chastity Concept** (Meme) suppresses **Sexual Desire** (Gene).
    - **Integrity Education** (Meme) suppresses **Greed** (Gene).
- **Cost**: Individuals produce psychological conflict, neurosis, repression (Freud's "Civilization and Its Discontents").

### Mechanism 2: Gene Rebellion (Instinct Breakout)

- **Definition**: Gene instincts break through meme constraints, causing social rules to fail.
- **Cases**:
    - **Cannibalism during Famine** (Survival instinct penetrates moral memes).
    - **Extramarital Affairs** under strict monogamy (Reproductive strategy penetrates legal memes).
- **Result**: Social disorder, system entropy increase.

### Mechanism 3: False Collusion (Sacralization)

- **Definition**: Memes use gene reward mechanisms (dopamine) to induce individuals to sacrifice for the group.
- **Mechanism**:
    - Genes set a reward circuit for "seeking recognition".
    - Memes define "sacrifice is glorious".
    - **Result**: Individuals gain huge dopamine rewards (sense of sublimity) when sacrificing, genes think they won (gained status), memes also won (gained spread).
- **Cases**: **Religious Martyrdom**, **Suicide Attacks**, **Excessive Consumption** (Filling dopamine gaps with material goods).

## 3.5 Death Mechanism: Evolution's Ultimate Move

### Core Proposition

**Death is not the end of life, but an efficient refresh mechanism invented by the system to counter the Second Law of Thermodynamics (Entropy Increase).**

From the perspective of gene group evolution, individual death has extremely high positive significance:

### 1. Niche Vacation and Gene Renewal

- **Finiteness Axiom**: Niches (resources) are limited.
- **Renewal Mechanism**: If old individuals do not die, new individuals (trial and error agents carrying mutant genes) have no living space.
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Death forcibly clears niches, allowing offspring more adapted to the environment to take the stage. **No death, no evolution.**

### 2. Countering Cognitive Hardening (System Heat Death)

- **Cognitive Hardening**: Over time, individual neural network paths will solidify (empiricism), making it difficult to accept new memes.
- **System Rigidity**: If a group consists of immortal individuals, its cultural memes will stop flowing, and system entropy tends to maximum (heat death).
- **Death Cleaning**: Death clears old brains rigidified by path dependence, introducing "blank slate" new brains to the system.

### 3. Altruistic Self-Destruction (Programmed Death)

- **Cell Apoptosis**: For organism health, damaged cells will actively initiate self-destruction programs.
- **Social Death**: During tribal conflicts or resource scarcity, old and weak individuals will actively sacrifice to preserve young genes carrying more future (e.g., Inuit custom of abandoning the elderly).
- **Conclusion**: At the level of group evolution, **individual death is often a necessary cost for group survival**.
