# Appendix C: Summary of Theorem System

## I. Lower Order Tao Theorems

### Theorem 1: Pyramid Structure Theorem
**Spontaneous order inevitably forms a pyramid hierarchy with "many bottom-level producers and few top-level controllers", where resources concentrate from bottom to top.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Natural Enrichment of Surplus Resources Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Social stratification presents a conical structure

### Theorem 2: Class Solidification Theorem
**The top layer will transfer power, resources, and advantages across generations. Opportunities for class mobility decrease over time, and barriers become harder.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Rational Profit-Seeking Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Intergenerational transmission of education, economy, and social capital

### Theorem 3: Placebo Channel Theorem
**The system will retain an extremely small probability of upward mobility channels, aiming to appease the bottom layer and suppress resistance, rather than truly breaking stratification.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Domestication Mechanism
- **Manifestation**: Selection systems, success myths, consumerism

### Theorem 4: Moral Instrument Theorem
**The core function of lower-order morality is to domesticate the bottom layer to accept the status quo and weaken resistance, serving the stability of the predatory system.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Legitimization Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Packaging obedience as virtue, viewing resistance as vice

### Theorem 5: Technological Efficiency Theorem
**Increments created by technology will be prioritized and monopolized by the top layer for predation, only increasing the scale and concealment of predation, without changing the essence of predation.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Unidirectional Resource Flow Theorem
- **Manifestation**: Technological progress exacerbates wealth gap

### Theorem 6: Endogenous Cycle Theorem
**Continuous intensification of internal predation → Serious imbalance in resource allocation → Survival pressure on the bottom layer → Social collapse and reconstruction.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dissipative Structure Survival Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Rise and fall of dynasties

## II. Higher Order Tao Theorems

### Theorem 7: Compliance Theorem
**Higher Order Tao does not deny underlying laws such as survival needs, limited resources, and group evolution, but reconstructs resource allocation and power rules.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meta-Axiom System
- **Core Logic**: Achieving better resource allocation through institutional design while respecting objective laws

### Theorem 8: Anti-Predation Theorem
**Abolishing "gratuitous possession by monopolizing means of production/power" through institutions, eliminating institutionalized predation within humans, retaining only equal exchange and reasonable division of labor benefits.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Predation for Profit Axiom
- **Core Logic**: Curbing predatory behavior through institutional design, ensuring fair distribution of resources

### Theorem 9: Group Advantage Theorem
**Investing incremental resources in universal education, medical care, innovation, infrastructure, and security, resulting in overall vitality, risk resistance, and long-term survival quality far higher than lower-order order.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Collaborative Value-Add Axiom
- **Core Logic**: Improving overall quality and ability of the group through public investment

### Theorem 10: Anti-Solidification Theorem
**Class mobility is based solely on individual ability, labor, and contribution, blocking intergenerational monopoly advantages and maintaining stable mobility.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Reverse Design of Class Solidification Theorem
- **Core Logic**: Establishing fair competition mechanisms, ensuring equal opportunities

### Theorem 11: Cycle Escape Theorem
**Eliminating the endogenous collapse motivation of "infinite upgrade of predation", resource allocation becomes sustainable, no longer falling into the cycle of Lower Order Tao.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Reverse Design of Endogenous Cycle Theorem
- **Core Logic**: Achieving balanced energy flow through institutional design

## III. Social Trophic Level Theory Theorems

### Theorem 12: Unidirectional Resource Flow Theorem
**In a spontaneous state without external rigid constraints, surplus resources naturally flow from the production layer to the accumulation layer; the wealth proportion of the accumulation layer increases monotonically over time.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Natural Enrichment of Surplus Resources Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Wealth gap continuously widens

### Theorem 13: Structural Stability Theorem
**The stability cycle of the predation-dominated structure is strictly negatively correlated with predation intensity and strictly positively correlated with the survival guarantee level of the production layer.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dissipative Structure Survival Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Higher predation intensity leads to more unstable structure

### Theorem 14: Transmission Layer Alienation Theorem
**Without rigid institutional constraints, the transmission layer will inevitably form a predatory interest alliance with the accumulation layer, abandoning the core functions of resource regulation and serving production.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Rational Profit-Seeking Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Bureaucratic corruption, institutional failure

### Theorem 15: Efficiency Limit Theorem
**The resource utilization efficiency of the predation-dominated structure has a rigid upper limit, and its efficiency improvement can only be achieved by increasing predation intensity on the production layer or squeezing the reasonable income of the transmission layer.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Unidirectional Resource Flow Theorem
- **Manifestation**: Weak economic growth, intensified social contradictions

### Theorem 16: Stagnant Steady State Theorem
**When the predation-dominated structure simultaneously meets three conditions: "Weak Predation Intensity (not breaking production layer survival baseline), Full Class Solidification (no upward mobility space), Strong Ideological Taming (dissolving resistance will)", it will form a low-level stagnant steady state.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Domestication Mechanism
- **Manifestation**: Society lacks vitality, innovation stagnates

### Theorem 17: Symbiotic Efficiency Theorem
**The resource utilization efficiency of the symbiosis-dominated structure has no rigid upper limit, and can achieve long-term exponential growth through the release of national innovation vitality, continuous improvement of collaboration efficiency, and technological breakthroughs.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Collaborative Value-Add Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Sustainable economic development, society full of vitality

### Theorem 18: Long-Term Evolutionary Competition Theorem
**In long-term evolutionary competition across groups and civilizations, the long-term survival probability, risk resistance, and development potential of the symbiosis-dominated structure are significantly higher than those of the predation-dominated structure.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Group Evolution Priority Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Symbiotic civilizations are more competitive

### Theorem 19: Dynamic Balance Theorem
**The symbiosis-dominated structure achieves dynamic balance between efficiency and fairness through institutional design, maintaining individual innovation motivation while ensuring social fairness and justice.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Collaborative Value-Add Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Unity of efficiency and fairness

### Theorem 20: Self-Optimization Theorem
**The symbiosis-dominated structure has the ability of self-optimization, able to continuously adapt to environmental changes and achieve sustainable development through institutional innovation and cultural evolution.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dual Inheritance System Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Continuous innovation of institutions and culture

## IV. Gene Group Evolution Theory Theorems

### Theorem 21: Dual Coding Synergy Theorem
**The co-evolution of genes and memes is the core driving force of human social evolution, interacting and shaping each other.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dual Coding Essence Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Genes provide physiological basis, memes shape social behavior

### Theorem 22: Meme Domestication Effectiveness Theorem
**Meme domestication is an effective mechanism for social systems to maintain order, coordinating individual behavior through non-violent means.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Domestication Mechanism Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Internalization of social norms, conscious coordination of individual behavior

### Theorem 23: Cognitive Breakthrough Theorem
**The cognitive layer is the highest dimension of evolution, achieving consciousness anti-domestication and cognitive sovereignty through dimensional reduction observation.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Negative Entropy Drive Logic Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Individual cognitive awakening, critical thinking about the system

### Theorem 24: Energy Transformation Theorem
**The stability of the social system depends on the effective transformation of individual energy into system energy, and false subjectivity is the key mechanism for achieving this transformation.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Negative Entropy Drive Logic Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Individuals contributing energy in the illusion of "autonomous choice"

### Theorem 25: Group Selection Theorem
**Group selection is an important mechanism of biological evolution, and traits beneficial to group survival will be retained and strengthened.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Group Evolution Priority Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Evolution of altruistic behavior, formation of group cohesion
